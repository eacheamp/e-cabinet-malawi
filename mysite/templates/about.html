About e-Cabinet Malawi

e-Cabinet uses web-based software and audio-visual equipment, ministers can take part remotely, and have often done so. Decisions made at the meetings can be e-mailed to interested parties or posted on a website even while the meeting is still going on. The average length of the weekly cabinet meetings was cut from 4 or 5 hours to between 30 and 90 minutes.

Members enter meeting room Members vote on motions one by one They then tally the votes costing time Or secret ballots require writing the votes on paper, which is tedious and wasteful The final decision is rendered after much time and resources are expended

The old way called for a count to determine Quorum, long discussions, edits, amendments, confusion, written ballots; leading to a waste of Cabinet Member time and resources. Why not use Technology to out advantage?
